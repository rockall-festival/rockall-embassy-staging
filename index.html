Rockall is a rock in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean. Just 25 by 30 metres, it occupies one of the roughest parts of the ocean. It is uninhabitable for humans and if it weren't for the surrounding natural resources, few would care for this barren peak of an extinct volcano.

As it is, the island is highly contested. Four countries - the UK, Denmark, Ireland and Iceland - have advanced a claim in recent years. But, the UN Law of the Seas states that noone can claim land that is not permanently habitable, and none of the claimants have been able to install a permanent population.

What if we would make it a habitable and liveable place? Since nobody owns the land, we could start imagining a new country, a new system and find ways to live our lives in a more sustainable way.
